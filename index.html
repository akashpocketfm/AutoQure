Where the Time Has Been Going

These are directional workstream estimates based on the visible project activity. They are not logged timesheets, but they explain why “tests written” has grown faster than “tests completely ready”.

Device stability and UiAutomator2 endurance~35%

Samsung M12 endurance, UiAutomator2 crashes, disconnects, resets, and repeated long-suite recovery have consumed a large share of the recent effort.

Coverage generation and feature expansion~25%

New test classes, regression depth, page objects, feature matrices, and suite growth are the visible coverage gains the team sees in repo count.

State management: cold, warm, seeded, payment~20%

Much of the automation complexity is making the same app work reliably across onboarding, warm-feed, seeded-auth, and monetization-sensitive states.

Reporting, orchestration, and suite plumbing~10%

Deployment-smoke orchestration, merged reports, per-phase artifacts, and guardrails are not flashy, but they are required for management-safe, one-command execution.

Blocked fixtures and monetization constraints~10%

Unlock, Social, Show Detail, and payment isolation depend on deterministic content, seeded users, and backend-safe flows, which slows promotion even when code exists.

Current Blockers and Why Ready Count Is Lower Than Repo Count

Higher-level explanation: the gap is now mostly in execution reliability and fixture determinism, not in our ability to write additional test code.

Technical / Device Constraints

  • The Samsung M12 is not reliably sustaining long runs of 30+ back-to-back Appium sessions without UiAutomator2 resets or phase splitting.
  • Deployment smoke exists architecturally, but warm and seeded phases still need split-phase hardening before they can be counted as management-safe.
  • Some time has been lost to AI/linter corruption of shared framework files, which then had to be restored and guarded.

Feature / Fixture Constraints

  • Unlock needs deterministic locked content or a seeded limited-access user to become promotion-ready.
  • Social needs a reliable comments surface on available content.
  • Show Detail still needs a stable, repeatable user navigation path on the current app/device flow.
  • Payments must remain sandboxed and isolated from the normal smoke users.

What Management Should Take Away

A concise leadership reading of where we stand right now.

What has materially improved

Since the earlier 19-test trusted smoke snapshot, the project has grown to 60 generated tests, 107 total repo tests, a 36-test fully green expanded smoke baseline, isolated payment sandbox automation, a deployment-smoke backbone, and a management-level feature matrix.

Why this still feels slower than expected

AI accelerated test writing, but the current bottleneck is converting those tests into stable real-device coverage across cold, warm, seeded, and payment-sensitive states on an unstable Samsung M12 execution environment.
